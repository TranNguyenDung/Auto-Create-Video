Growth mindset vs fixed mindset.

There are two kinds of people in this world…

Those who believe they were born the way they are.

And those who believe they can become different.

Fixed mindset…

says talent is innate.

That you are born good at something or not.

That everything is predetermined.

Growth mindset…

says you can learn.

That you can change.

That you can rise.

The difference lies in one word: "yet".

A fixed mindset person says "I'm not good at this."

A growth mindset person says "I'm not good at this yet."

Just one word "yet"…

changes your entire attitude toward life.

When you think you cannot…

you will not try.

And when you do not try…

you truly cannot.

A self-fulfilling prophecy.

A fixed mindset person fears failure.

Because failure is proof…

that they are not good enough.

But a growth mindset person…

sees failure as a lesson.

As a step on the path of growth.

Psychologist Carol Dweck spent her life studying this.

She discovered that…

how you think about yourself…

determines how you live your life.

Look at the most successful people…

they are not the ones who have never failed.

They are the ones who never stop learning.

Never stop adapting.

Never stop growing.

Thomas Edison failed thousands of times…

but he did not call it failure.

He called it finding ways that did not work.

And eventually he found the way that worked.

That is a growth mindset.

You can train it every day.

Start by changing how you talk to yourself.

Instead of "I cannot do it"…

say "I cannot do it yet".

Instead of "I'm bad at this"…

say "I need to learn more about this".

Instead of "I've lost"…

say "I've learned something".

Small changes in language…

create big changes in mindset.

Because your brain…

is like a muscle.

The more it is exercised…

the stronger it becomes.

Do not say "I'm not that kind of person".

Ask "how can I become that kind of person".

Do not box yourself in.

Let tomorrow…

be a better version of today.

Because a person…

is not a finished statue.

But a work in progress.

Each day is a chisel stroke.

Each failure is a contour line.

Each lesson is a new edge.

And in the end…

that statue will become beautiful.

If you believe…

that you can change.

Then you have already changed from that moment.

Choose a growth mindset.

Because it is the only mindset…

that helps you become the best version of yourself.
