Looking back to cherish the present.

There is a paradox in life.
We often don't value what we have.
Until we lose it.

The young want to grow up fast.
The grown want to be young again.
The single want to get married.
The married want to be free.

People always look toward what they don't have.
And forget what they already have.

But if you stop…
and look back at the past…

You'll see how far you've come.
How many difficulties you've overcome.
How much you've grown compared to before.

Things that once made you cry…
now are just a smile.
Things that once scared you…
now are trivial.
Things that once were mountains…
now are just pebbles.

That is the power of time.
And also the lesson of growth.

Look back five years.
Where were you?
What were you doing?
What were you worrying about?

So much has changed.
Perhaps you've lost a loved one.
Perhaps you've achieved a major goal.
Perhaps you've completely changed your life.

No matter what…
you are still here.
Still breathing.
Still living.
Still have the chance to love and be loved.

That alone is a miracle.

But people forget this miracle.
We are too busy with worries.
Too busy with goals.
So busy we forget that being alive is a gift.

Practice the habit of looking back.
Every night before sleep…
take a few minutes.

Look back at the day that passed.
Not to judge.
But to appreciate.

You had a good meal.
You met someone.
You laughed at least once.
You did something good.

These small things…
if gathered together…
form a life worth living.

The poet Rumi once said…
what you are seeking…
is also seeking you.

Happiness is not far away.
It is right here.
In this moment.
In this breath.

Just look back…
and realize you already have enough.
Enough to be happy.

Don't wait until you're wealthy to be happy.
Don't wait until you're successful to be satisfied.
Don't wait until you're perfect to love.

Happiness is a choice.
And it begins by looking back…
to see what you already have.

Write down three things you're grateful for each day.
Three small things.
Three ordinary things.
Three things you usually take for granted.

A cup of morning coffee.
A friend who texted to check on you.
A rainy afternoon sitting in a warm home.

And then you'll see…
this life is actually very beautiful.
Beautiful in the most ordinary things.

Looking back is not to regret.
Looking back is to see how fortunate you are.
And to cherish the present more.

Because today's present…
will be tomorrow's memory.
Live so that when you look back…
you smile.
