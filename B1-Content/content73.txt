The healing power of nature.

Humans have drifted away from nature.

We live in concrete buildings.

Sit in air-conditioned rooms.

Stare at bright screens.

We forget that…

we are part of nature.

And nature has healing power.

No medicine needed.

No doctor needed.

Just step outside.

A study shows…

just two hours in nature each week…

is enough to significantly improve health.

Two hours.

One hundred twenty minutes.

Less than a movie.

Walk in the park.

Sit under a tree.

Walk barefoot on grass.

Feel the earth beneath your feet.

It's called "forest bathing"…

a healing practice from Japan.

Not bathing in water.

But bathing in fresh air.

In the green of leaves.

In the sound of birds.

When you are in nature…

your body relaxes.

Your heart rate slows.

Blood pressure drops.

Cortisol – the stress hormone – also decreases.

You breathe deeper.

Your lungs receive clean air.

Not city smoke and dust.

But the scent of soil.

Of leaves.

Of flowers and grass.

Every breath is medicine.

Sunlight also heals.

Vitamin D from the early sun…

is good for bones.

Good for mood.

Helps you sleep better.

Fifteen minutes of sun each day…

is enough for your body to absorb.

But nature doesn't just heal the body.

It heals the soul.

When you watch a sunset…

you feel how small you are.

Your worries…

become small too.

When you listen to a flowing stream…

you learn patience.

Water doesn't rush.

It flows until it reaches its destination.

When you observe a tree…

you learn endurance.

The tree stands through storms.

Through cold winters.

And every spring it buds and sprouts new leaves.

Nature teaches you…

everything has its own rhythm.

No need to hurry.

No need to compare.

Flowers bloom in their own season.

So do you.

You can bring nature indoors.

A small potted plant on your desk.

A few flowers in the room.

The color green soothes the nerves.

It helps you focus.

And feel more at peace.

On weekends, escape the city.

Go somewhere far.

Where there's no phone signal.

Where there's only trees and sky.

There, you can hear your inner voice.

The voice that has been drowned out…

by traffic horns…

by phone notifications…

by the rush of life.

Nature gives you space to breathe.

To rest.

To simply exist.

Give yourself that gift.

Step outside.

Breathe.

And let nature embrace you.

For you are a child of the earth and sky.

And the earth and sky always await you with open arms.
