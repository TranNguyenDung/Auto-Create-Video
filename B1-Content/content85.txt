Lessons from people who lived past 90.

They have lived nearly a century.

They have witnessed war and peace.
They have been through prosperity and recession.
They have loved and lost.
They have laughed and cried.

Those who live past ninety…
are living books.

If you ask them the secret to a long life…
you'll be surprised by the answer.

It's not in medicine.
It's not in dieting.
It lies in the simplest things.

First…
don't worry too much.

Most long-lived people…
have one thing in common.
They don't worry about small things.

When you live to be ninety…
you realize…
most of the things you worried about…
never happened.

And if they did happen…
it wasn't as bad as you thought.

Second…
stay connected with people.

Long-lived people…
have a strong social network.
They have family.
They have friends.
They have community.

Loneliness kills faster than smoking.
Connection nourishes the soul.

A grandmother who lived to a hundred once said…
my secret is always having someone to love.

Third…
move every day.

Elderly people who live long…
are not heavy exercisers.
They are people who move gently.
Every day.

Gardening.
Walking.
Climbing stairs.
Cleaning the house.

Movement is life.
Stopping movement is stopping life.

Okinawa in Japan…
has the most centenarians in the world.
They don't go to the gym.
They just move throughout the day.

And they have a saying…
ikigai.
The reason to wake up each morning.

Fourth…
eat a little less.

Long-lived people…
usually eat until eighty percent full.
Not overeating.
Not too much meat.
Plenty of vegetables.
Plenty of fish.
And drinking tea every day.

Fifth and most important…
know how to let go.

Those who live to ninety…
have learned a valuable lesson.
There are things that cannot be changed.
There are things not worth holding onto.
Hatred.
Regret.
Anger.

They are all burdens.
And old age has no room for burdens.

They forgive.
They forget.
They live in the present.

They don't try to control everything.
They accept the flow of life.
And they find that…
when you stop fighting life…
life becomes gentle with you.

There's a question people often ask the elderly.
If you could live again…
what would you do differently?

The answer is often…
I would worry less.
I would love more.
I would enjoy every moment.
Because life is short.
Much shorter than you think.

Learn from them.
While you are still young.
While you still have time.
Don't wait until you're ninety…
to understand these things.
