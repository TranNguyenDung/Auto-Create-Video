Overcoming imposter syndrome.

Have you ever…

felt that you do not deserve it?

That your success is just luck.

That one day…

people will find out…

you are actually not as good as they think.

If you have…

hello…

you are not alone.

This is imposter syndrome.

And it is silently stealing your happiness.

Imposter syndrome…

is the feeling that you are a fraud.

Even though you have achieved things…

you still think you do not deserve it.

You think everyone is mistaken.

That you were just lucky.

That you will be exposed.

Sound familiar?

Albert Einstein once said…

"I do not deserve people's admiration."

Maya Angelou once said…

"I have written dozens of books…

but I am still afraid every time I write…

that people will find out I do not know how to write."

Even the greatest people…

have doubted themselves.

So why would you not?

This syndrome often stems from…

expectations that are too high.

From perfect standards.

From the fear of not being good enough.

But the truth is…

no one is perfect.

Everyone has shortcomings.

Everyone has gaps.

The difference is…

successful people accept that.

But people with imposter syndrome…

hide it.

Do you know how to overcome it?

Start by acknowledging it.

Say it out loud: "I feel like an imposter."

When you admit it…

you take away its power.

You no longer have to hide.

Then, look at the facts.

Look at what you have done.

The projects you have completed.

The difficulties you have overcome.

The value you have created.

These things are not luck.

They are your effort.

Your intelligence.

Your being.

Practice saying "I deserve it".

Whether you believe it or not…

say it every day.

In front of the mirror.

To yourself.

One more important thing…

do not compare yourself to others.

Everyone's journey is different.

You are somewhere on your own path.

And that is completely okay.

Learn to celebrate small successes.

Instead of saying "it was just luck"…

say "I worked hard for this".

Instead of saying "anyone could do it"…

say "I did something difficult".

Imposter syndrome will never completely disappear.

It may come back.

In moments of insecurity.

When you face new challenges.

But next time…

you will know how to face it.

You will say: "Hello, I know you.

But I will not let you control me anymore."

Because the truth is…

you are not an imposter.

You are someone who is learning.

Growing.

Becoming better every day.

And that…

makes you completely deserving.

Of every success in life.
