Secrets to healthy eating.

You are what you eat.

That saying is not a metaphor.

It is a biological truth.

Every bite of food that enters your body…

becomes a part of you.

Your energy.

Your mood.

Your health.

All stem from food.

So healthy eating…

is not a temporary diet.

It is a way of life.

But healthy eating…

doesn't mean bland.

Doesn't mean only eating vegetables.

Doesn't mean starving.

It is about balance.

Start with the simplest things.

Drink enough water.

Your body needs water to function.

Two liters a day.

Not soda.

Not coffee.

Filtered water.

Pure.

Clean.

Drink a glass right when you wake up.

Your body is thirsty after a long sleep.

Next is green vegetables.

Half your plate…

should be vegetables and fruits.

They provide vitamins.

Minerals.

Fiber.

The more varied the colors…

the richer the nutrition.

Don't fear carbohydrates.

Carbs are energy.

But choose good carbs.

Brown rice instead of white rice.

Sweet potatoes instead of white bread.

Oatmeal instead of sugary cereal.

The difference is in quality.

Protein is equally important.

Protein from fish, white meat, eggs…

from beans, nuts, and dairy.

Your body needs protein to build muscle.

To recover.

To function.

Every meal should have a protein source.

And don't forget good fats.

Not all fats are bad.

Olive oil.

Avocado.

Nuts.

Salmon.

They are good for your heart.

Good for your brain.

Good for your skin.

Healthy eating also means eating at the right times.

Don't skip breakfast.

Don't eat too late.

Your stomach also needs rest.

Eat slowly.

Chew thoroughly.

Savor the flavor.

Your brain needs twenty minutes…

to realize you are full.

If you eat too fast…

you will eat more than necessary.

Reducing sugar is one of the best things you can do.

Sugar is addictive.

It makes you want to eat more.

It makes you tired after the rush.

Cut back gradually.

Replace pastries with fruit.

Replace soda with water with lemon slices.

Your body will thank you.

You don't have to change all at once.

Change little by little.

This week, change one meal.

Next week, change another meal.

Gradually…

your body will get used to it.

And in the end…

you will choose healthy food…

naturally.

The secret is not in austerity.

It lies in love for your body.

When you love your body…

you will give it the best food.

Not because you have to.

But because you want to.

And that is when healthy eating…

is no longer an effort.

It becomes a joy.
