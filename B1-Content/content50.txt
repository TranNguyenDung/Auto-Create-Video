The art of negotiation.

Life is a negotiation.

Don't you think?

From buying an item…

to negotiating a salary.

From persuading your child to study…

to agreeing on a contract.

It is all negotiation.

Yet many people…

are afraid to negotiate.

They think negotiation is a fight.

Whoever is tougher wins.

But the truth is not that.

True negotiation…

is finding a solution that benefits both sides.

Not you win, I lose.

But both win together.

The first principle…

is to know clearly what you want.

Before entering any negotiation…

ask yourself:

What truly matters to me?

What are my limits?

What can I concede?

When you know what you want…

you will be more confident.

And won't be led astray.

The second principle…

is to understand the other person.

What do they want?

What do they fear?

What drives them?

When you understand them…

you can find common ground.

And propose suitable solutions.

Remember…

emotions can cloud reason.

In negotiation…

whoever stays calm…

has the advantage.

Don't let emotions take over.

Focus on interests.

Not on positions.

Position is what people say they want.

Interest is what they truly need.

Dig deeper than the position…

and you will find the real interest.

From there you can negotiate.

The third principle…

is to listen more than you talk.

Most people…

talk too much in negotiations.

They want to prove themselves.

They want to persuade.

But smart people…

listen.

Because when you listen…

you gather information.

And information is power.

Ask questions.

Be curious.

Let the other person talk.

You will be surprised by what they reveal.

The fourth principle…

is to be willing to walk away.

If there is no good deal…

it is better to have no deal.

The willingness to walk away…

gives you the greatest power in negotiation.

It shows you are not desperate.

You have other options.

You are not dependent.

And when you can walk away…

you can truly negotiate.

Negotiation is not war.

It is a dance.

Where both find a common rhythm.

Remember…

the goal is not to win.

But to reach the best agreement.

For both sides.

That is the true art of negotiation.
