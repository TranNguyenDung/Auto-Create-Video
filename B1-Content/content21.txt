Patience - A precious virtue.

Everything takes time.

A seed cannot become a tree in a day.

A child cannot grow up overnight.

But modern people…

always want everything fast.

Fast food.

Fast love.

Fast success.

Everything must be immediate.

But there are things…

that cannot be rushed.

The more you rush, the more you break.

The faster you go, the more mistakes you make.

Patience is the most precious virtue…

that few people still hold onto.

Are you patient?

Try looking at how you stand in line.

How you wait.

How you face delays.

Patience is not passive waiting.

It is waiting with a positive attitude.

It is believing that…

everything is unfolding in its own way.

The ancients had a saying…

"ripe fruit falls by itself."

Implying that…

patience brings good results.

No one succeeds overnight.

Behind every success…

are years of persistence.

Are sleepless nights.

Are failures.

Are times of getting back up.

Bill Gates did not build Microsoft in a day.

Steve Jobs did not create the iPhone overnight.

They were patient.

Through every failure.

Through every experiment.

Through every rejection.

And eventually they made it.

Patience is when…

you continue even without seeing results.

You still plant seeds…

even when no sprout appears.

You still run…

even when the finish line is not in sight.

Because you know…

every step brings you closer.

A story about the Chinese bamboo…

After planting, it must be watered and fertilized for four years.

The bamboo barely grows at all.

But in the fifth year…

it shoots up over 30 meters in six weeks.

The question is…

did the bamboo grow in six weeks?

Or did it grow over four years?

The answer is…

it was growing for four years.

But underground.

Where no one could see.

Its root system had spread…

to support the 30-meter height.

You are the same.

Your efforts today…

may seem to produce no results.

But they are growing underground.

Building the foundation.

Preparing for the breakthrough.

So…

be patient.

Do not give up when you see no results.

Do not stop planting seeds.

Do not stop watering.

The harvest…

will come at its own time.

And you will see…

every wait was worth it.

Because the best things…

always come to those who are patient.

Patience is not instinct.

It is a virtue.

It can be trained.

It can be nurtured.

And once you have it…

you will find life lighter.

Because you are no longer rushing.

You know that…

everything has its time.

And that time will come.

If you are patient enough to wait.
