Secrets to keeping the flame alive in love.

Love is not a destination.

It is a journey.

When you first fall in love…

everything burns bright.

But time…

can make the flame fade.

Not because love is gone.

But because of familiarity.

So how do you keep the flame of love burning?

The answer lies in the small daily things.

Don't let familiarity kill romance.

Still send loving texts even after years together.

Still say thank you.

Still look at each other with tender eyes.

Those small things…

are the fuel for the flame of love.

The first secret…

make time for each other.

Life is busy.

Work.

Children.

Bills.

Responsibilities.

Everything pulls you apart.

But if you don't have time for each other…

love will slowly die.

Set aside at least fifteen minutes each day…

just to talk, no phones, no TV.

Have a date every week…

even if it's just a walk together.

The second secret…

never stop discovering each other.

You think you know everything about them?

No one truly knows everything about anyone.

People always change.

Every day there is a new version.

Be curious about each other.

Ask new questions.

Discover unknown facets.

The third secret…

keep your own space.

Loving doesn't mean merging into one.

Each person needs their own space.

Their own hobbies.

Their own friends.

When you have a rich personal life…

you bring more interesting things back to the relationship.

The fourth secret…

learn to forgive.

No one is perfect.

There will be times they disappoint you.

There will be times you hurt them.

Forgiveness is the glue that holds love together.

Don't hold onto hurts.

Speak them out.

Forgive.

And move on.

The fifth secret…

never stop showing affection.

Many couples think…

once you love each other, you don't need to say it.

But no…

love needs to be nourished through words and actions.

A surprise hug.

A small gift for no occasion.

A compliment on an ordinary day.

All of these keep the flame alive.

And above all…

choose to love each other every day.

Love is not just a feeling.

It is a choice.

You choose to stay.

You choose to love.

You choose to nurture.

Every day.

Not because you have to.

But because you want to.

Keeping the flame of love alive is not hard.

If you remember that…

love is like a garden.

It needs to be watered every day.

Not just once and then done.
