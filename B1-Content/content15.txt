How to build confidence.

Confidence is not something you are born with.

Nor is it something you can buy.

Confidence is built…

day by day…

step by step…

experience by experience.

Many people think…

you must be confident before you dare to act.

But the truth is the opposite.

You must act…

to build confidence.

Confidence is the result of doing…

not a prerequisite.

Albert Einstein once said…

do not worry about your failures.

Learn from them and keep going.

And that is how confidence is built.

The first step…

prepare.

No one is confident facing something completely unfamiliar.

Confidence comes with preparation.

The more you know…

the more confident you are.

The more you practice…

the more steady you become.

So if you lack confidence…

ask yourself: have I prepared enough?

The second step…

start with small things.

Do not try to conquer Everest on the first try.

Climb a small hill first.

Do what you fear…

but at the mildest level.

Talk to a stranger.

Ask a question in a meeting.

Raise your hand to speak in class.

Every time you overcome a small fear…

you send a signal to your brain:

"I can do this."

And your confidence grows from there.

The third step…

stop comparing.

One of the biggest enemies of confidence…

is comparison.

You see others succeed…

and feel inferior.

But you only see the tip of the iceberg.

You do not see their process.

You do not see their failures.

You do not see the effort they put in.

Focus on your own journey.

Measure your progress…

against yourself yesterday.

The fourth step…

accept that you do not need to be perfect.

Confident people are not those who never make mistakes.

Confident people are those…

who dare to be wrong…

dare to learn…

and dare to try again.

They do not let one mistake define them.

They see it as a lesson.

And move on.

The final step…

take care of yourself.

A healthy body produces a strong mind.

Get enough sleep.

Exercise.

Eat well.

When you feel good about your body…

confidence naturally comes.

Building confidence is a journey.

Not a destination.

Some days you will feel strong.

Some days you will doubt yourself.

Both are normal.

What matters is…

you keep moving.

Because every step…

no matter how small…

is bringing you closer…

to the most confident version of yourself.
