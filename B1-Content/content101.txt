Lessons on humility.

In a world that celebrates the ego…
humility has become a rare quality.

People teach their children to be confident.
To assert themselves.
To speak their worth.

And they forget…
humility is true strength.

Humility is not lowering yourself.
Humility is seeing the truth about yourself.
Seeing that you are not perfect.
Seeing that you still have much to learn.
Seeing that you are just a small part of a vast universe.

There is a story about the great scientist Albert Einstein.
A student asked him…
why do you always say you know nothing?
Einstein replied…
the more I know…
the more I realize how little I know.

That is the philosophy of the wise.
The wider the circle of knowledge…
the larger the contact with the unknown.

The ignorant are often complacent.
The knowledgeable are always humble.

Look at the rice plant.
When it hasn't yet formed grain…
it stands tall, proud.
But when the grain is full and heavy…
it bows its head.

Those with true knowledge understand…
they don't know everything.
Those with true talent see…
they still have much to learn.
Those with true value don't need to prove it.

Humility helps us open our hearts.
When you are humble…
you are willing to learn from everyone.
From the old, the young.
From the successful, the failed.
From children too.

Every person has something to teach you.
If you are humble enough to listen.

Humility also helps us overcome failure.
The arrogant will crumble when they fail.
Because they cannot accept their weakness.

The humble will get back up.
Because they know failure is part of learning.
Nothing to be ashamed of.

Humility is the foundation of all other virtues.
The humble are truly courageous.
Because they dare to face their weaknesses.
They dare to admit mistakes.
They dare to apologize first.

The humble are truly strong.
Because they don't need to prove themselves.
They don't need to be better than others.
They just need to be themselves.

And strangely enough…
it's when you stop trying to prove yourself…
that others see your value.

Like a flower in the garden.
It doesn't need to tell anyone it's beautiful.
It just quietly blooms.
And people come to admire it.

Practice humility every day.
When praised…
say thank you and remember…
many people have helped you reach where you are today.

When successful…
remember those who supported you.
When you have knowledge…
share it gently.
Not boasting.
Not condescending.

Living humbly doesn't make you smaller.
It makes you more noble.
In the eyes of the wise…
humility is the highest beauty.

And in this life…
there is nothing more admirable…
than a great soul that always bows its head.
