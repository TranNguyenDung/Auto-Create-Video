How to build trust.

Trust is like a blank sheet of paper.

Once crumpled…

even if smoothed…

the creases remain.

But without trust…

every relationship becomes fragile.

So how do you build trust?

The answer starts with small things.

Even the smallest promise must be kept.

If you say you will call back at eight…

call back at exactly eight.

If you say you will do something…

do exactly as promised.

Consistency…

is the foundation of trust.

People trust you not because you speak well.

But because you do exactly what you say.

Words and actions must go together.

Someone who says one thing and does another…

cannot be trusted.

Trust is also built on honesty.

Honest even when no one is watching.

Honest even when the truth embarrasses you.

Because an unpleasant truth…

is still better than a pleasant lie.

The ancients said…

once untrustworthy…

forever doubted.

Just one lie…

can destroy years of building.

So…

value your words.

Do not promise if you are not sure.

Do not say it if you do not intend to do it.

Trust also comes from being willing to listen.

When someone confides in you…

they place their trust in you.

Do not betray that.

Keep their secrets.

Give them a safe space.

And one important thing…

trust takes time.

It cannot be rushed.

Cannot be forced.

It grows slowly…

through every small interaction.

Through every time you prove yourself trustworthy.

Be patient.

Do not demand others trust you immediately.

Let them see…

through your actions.

Trust is also a two-way street.

You want people to trust you…

first you must trust them.

Trust in their good intentions.

Trust in their abilities.

When you give trust…

you usually receive trust in return.

And finally…

know how to forgive.

No one is perfect.

Others may have disappointed you.

But if they sincerely make amends…

give them a chance.

Because trust…

is like a tree.

Its branches may break in a storm.

But if cared for…

it will sprout again.

And one day…

it will provide shade.

Building trust is a journey.

It is daily patience.

But its reward…

is lasting relationships.

Where you can be yourself.

And not fear being hurt.
