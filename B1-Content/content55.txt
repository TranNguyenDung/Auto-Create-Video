Lessons from great entrepreneurs.

There is one thing all great entrepreneurs have in common…

They didn't start with the question: how to get rich.

They started with the question: what problem can I solve?

Steve Jobs once said…

stay hungry, stay foolish.

He didn't build Apple to become a billionaire.

He built Apple to change the world.

And that is the first lesson.

Work for a purpose, not for money.

Money is a consequence.

Not the destination.

The second lesson is from Walt Disney.

He was once fired for lacking ideas.

Before creating the world's largest entertainment empire.

He said…

the best way to get started is to stop talking and begin doing.

Many people talk too much.

They discuss plans.

They paint visions.

But they don't act.

The third lesson is from Warren Buffett.

One of the richest people in the world…

but lives more simply than you'd think.

He spends 80% of his time reading and thinking.

He teaches that…

the best investment is investing in yourself.

No skill is a waste.

No knowledge is superfluous.

The fourth lesson is from Oprah Winfrey.

Born in poverty…

raised in deprivation…

but became one of the most powerful women.

She said…

turn your wounds into strength.

Difficulties are not barriers.

They are素材 for success.

The fifth lesson is from Elon Musk.

A man who dares to think the unthinkable.

Electric cars. Reusable rockets. Sending people to Mars.

He said…

if something is important…

you should do it even if the odds are against you.

Because if you don't…

the chance of success is zero.

The sixth lesson is from Jack Ma.

Rejected 30 times when applying for jobs.

Knew nothing about technology.

Yet founded Alibaba.

He said…

today is difficult.

Tomorrow will be even more difficult.

But the day after tomorrow will be beautiful.

Most people give up tomorrow.

Great entrepreneurs…

are not smarter than you.

Not more talented than you.

Not richer than you when they started.

They just had one thing…

they never gave up.

They failed more than you think.

They stumbled more than you imagine.

But they always got back up.

And that is the greatest lesson.

Success doesn't come from never failing.

It comes from never giving up.
