How to reduce stress.

Stress is a part of life.

It can't be completely avoided.

Nor should it be.

A little stress keeps you alert.

Helps you focus.

Helps you get things done.

The problem arises…

when stress persists.

When it becomes chronic.

When it stays with you even while resting.

That's when you need solutions.

The first way…

recognize the stress.

Are you clenching your teeth?

Are you tensing your shoulders?

Are you breathing shallowly?

Stop for a second.

Notice the signals from your body.

Awareness is the first step.

Then…

breathe deeply.

Inhale for four seconds.

Hold for four seconds.

Exhale for six seconds.

Long breaths activate the parasympathetic nervous system.

It tells your body…

everything is safe.

Do this three times.

You will feel different.

Another way…

move your body.

When you're stressed, your body produces cortisol.

And the only way to burn off cortisol…

is to move.

Walk briskly for ten minutes.

Jump in place.

Stretch.

Anything that gets you moving.

Your body needs to release that energy.

Writing things down also works.

When stress takes over…

your mind is spinning.

Write it all down on paper.

No need for order.

No need for beautiful sentences.

Just write.

When you write it out…

you bring it from inside your head to the outside.

It's no longer an invisible demon.

It's words on a page.

And you can look at it objectively.

Focus on what you can control.

A lot of stress comes from…

worrying about things beyond your control.

The weather.

What others think.

The past.

The future.

Ask yourself…

what can I do about this?

If there's nothing you can do…

let it go.

If there is something you can do…

do it now.

Action is the antidote to anxiety.

Talk to someone.

Stress is heavier when you keep it inside.

Share it with a friend.

With a family member.

Just speaking it out…

already lightens the load by half.

The other person doesn't need to solve the problem.

They just need to listen.

Sometimes you don't need solutions.

You just need to be heard.

Get enough sleep.

Lack of sleep makes you more prone to stress.

Your tolerance decreases.

Small things annoy you.

Six hours of sleep…

is not enough.

Seven to eight hours is ideal.

Reduce caffeine and sugar.

They create fake energy.

Up and then down.

When you crash…

you feel exhausted and more stressed.

Cut back gradually.

You'll be surprised at the difference.

Make time for hobbies.

Read.

Listen to music.

Paint.

Garden.

Anything you love.

Hobbies aren't a waste of time.

They're how you recharge.

Finally…

practice letting go.

Not everything matters.

Not every fight is worth having.

Learn to say no.

To unnecessary tasks.

To people who drain you.

To unrealistic expectations.

Life is fragile.

Stress comes and goes.

But you remain.

Take care of yourself.

Breathe.

And remember…

everything will pass.
