Meditation for beginners.

Meditation.

A word that sounds distant.

Mystical.

Difficult.

But meditation is actually very simple.

So simple…

you can do it right now.

Right where you are.

Right in this moment.

Meditation is not about sitting cross-legged…

breathing strangely…

and saying "om" like in the movies.

Meditation is stopping.

Just stopping.

And observing.

In modern life…

we are always running.

Running to the past with regret.

Running to the future with anxiety.

Running from one thought to another.

We never stop.

Meditation is the moment you stop running.

You sit down.

And simply be present.

Start with three minutes each day.

Just three minutes.

Everyone has three minutes.

Find a quiet place.

Sit comfortably.

No need for the full lotus position.

A chair is fine.

A bed works too.

Close your eyes.

And bring your attention to your breath.

Feel the air entering your nose.

Feel your chest rising.

Feel your belly expanding and contracting.

Don't try to control your breath.

Just let it be natural.

You are only the observer.

And then… your mind will wander.

It will think about work.

About dinner.

About a conversation yesterday.

That is normal.

It is not a failure.

When you notice your mind has wandered…

gently bring it back.

Without judgment.

Without anger.

Gently.

Like guiding a lost child home.

Keep doing this.

Over and over again.

Each time you bring your mind back…

you are training your focus.

Like exercising a muscle.

A little every day.

Gradually…

you will see the difference.

After a week…

you notice you are less stressed.

After a month…

you control your emotions better.

After three months…

you see things more clearly.

Meditation does not make problems disappear.

But it changes how you face them.

You no longer react automatically.

You pause for a second.

And choose how to respond.

In that pause…

is freedom.

Meditation also helps you sleep better.

At night, lying in bed…

breathe for a few minutes.

Your mind settles.

Sleep comes naturally.

And meditation is free.

No app needed.

No course needed.

No equipment needed.

Just you.

And your breath.

The hardest part is starting.

But if you are reading these lines…

pause for a minute.

Close your eyes.

Breathe in deeply.

Breathe out slowly.

And repeat two more times.

Done.

You just meditated for ten seconds.

See…

it's simpler than you think.

Tomorrow…

do it for three minutes.

And the next day…

five minutes.

You don't need to become a meditation master.

Just spend a few minutes each day…

sitting still.

Listening to yourself.

Because in this noisy world…

silence is the most precious gift.
