Yoga and mental health.

When you think of yoga…

you think of strange contortionist poses.

Your body twisted like a paperclip.

But yoga is not just about postures.

It is a connection.

Between body and mind.

Between breath and movement.

Between yourself and the present.

Each pose is a story.

Each breath is a moment.

And mental health…

is built from those moments.

Stress is the number one enemy of modern people.

It lives in tense muscles.

In raised shoulders.

In clenched jaws.

Yoga helps release all of that.

When you stretch your body…

you also stretch your mind.

When you relax your shoulders…

you also let go of burdens.

The simplest poses…

often bring the greatest benefits.

Child's pose.

You kneel.

Fold forward.

Forehead touches the floor.

That is the pose of letting go.

In that pose…

you tell your body…

everything is okay.

You can rest.

Mountain pose.

Stand tall.

Feet grounded.

Arms at your sides.

You are as steady as a mountain.

No wind can shake you.

Warrior pose.

Step wide.

Arms extended.

Eyes looking forward.

You are strong.

Resilient.

Ready to face every challenge.

Each pose teaches you a lesson.

Yoga is not a competition.

You don't need to touch your toes.

You don't need to stand on your head.

You just need to give your best effort.

And accept where you are.

That is a lesson in self-compassion.

Breath is the heart of yoga.

In yoga, breath guides movement.

No breath…

no yoga.

When you focus on your breath…

you cannot think about the past.

You cannot worry about the future.

You can only be here.

Right now.

That is meditation in motion.

Yoga also balances hormones.

Reduces cortisol – the stress hormone.

Increases serotonin – the happiness hormone.

Helps you sleep well.

Eat well.

Feel more alive.

People who have practiced yoga for years…

have a peaceful look on their faces.

Not because they are numb.

But because they have learned to move through storms…

without being swept away.

You can start at home.

With a mat.

With online tutorial videos.

Five minutes each morning.

Before starting a new day.

Your body wakes up.

Your mind clear.

You step into the new day with calmness.

Or five minutes in the evening.

To release all negative energy.

To relax your body.

To prepare your mind for sleep.

Yoga does not discriminate by age.

Does not discriminate by gender.

Does not discriminate by body type.

Anyone can practice.

As long as you want to.

Try one session.

Feel your body after practice.

Feel the lightness.

And you will understand…

why yoga is not just exercise.

It is a way of life.

A way to love yourself.

A way to come home.
