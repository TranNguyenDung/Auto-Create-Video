A message to myself 10 years from now.

Imagine…
you are sitting right here.
Writing a letter to yourself.
To the person you will become ten years from now.

Ten years.
A span of time enough to change everything.

Perhaps you are older now.
Hair touched with gray.
Skin lined with wrinkles.

But I hope…
your heart is still young.

I hope…
you still know how to dream.
Still know how to love.
Still know how to cry at a good movie.
Still know how to laugh at something simple.

I know…
ten years hasn't been easy.

Perhaps you have endured losses.
Loved ones may no longer be here.
Dreams may have shattered.
Paths may have turned in different directions.

But I hope you don't become bitter.

Don't let wounds make you callous.
Don't let disappointments make you lose faith.
Don't let losses make you forget love.

Remember…
life is not what happens to you.
It's how you respond to it.

I want to tell you this.

Don't forget your core values.
Don't trade your conscience for gain.
Don't chase fame and forget family.
Don't let work devour your life.

When you look back…
you'll see the things you once thought important…
amounted to nothing.
And the things you once took for granted…
turned out to be everything.

I hope…
you still keep the habit of reading.
Because in books there are answers.
But more importantly…
in books there are questions.

I hope…
you still wake up early to watch the sunrise.
Still make time to walk alone.
Still know how to be silent to listen to your inner voice.

I hope…
you have learned to forgive.
Forgive those who hurt you.
And most importantly…
forgive yourself.

You don't have to be perfect.
You just need to be the best person you can be.

I hope…
you have a few true friends.
Not a thousand online acquaintances.
But a few you can call in the middle of the night.
And they will answer.

I hope…
you have found meaningful work.
It doesn't have to make you rich.
But it must make you wake up with excitement.

If not…
don't be afraid to change.
It's never too late to start over.

And finally…
I want to tell you…
thank you.
Thank you for not giving up.
Thank you for continuing to walk.
Even on days when you wanted to let everything go.

You have done well.
Even if no one tells you that.
I'm telling you now.

Keep living.
Keep loving.
Keep hoping.

And ten years from now…
when you read this letter again…
I hope you will smile.
And say to yourself.

I have lived a life worth living.
Not perfect.
But true.
And full of meaning.

To you, ten years from now…
I love you.
Remember that.
