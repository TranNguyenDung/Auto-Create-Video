How to find your passion.

There is a question…

that almost everyone has asked themselves.

What is my passion?

You may have sat for hours…

thinking about it…

and still not found the answer.

But the truth is…

passion is not something you find.

It is something you build.

Many people think…

passion is a lightning bolt from the sky.

A moment of revelation…

like in a Hollywood movie.

But life is not like the movies.

Passion comes from experimentation.

From daring to try new things.

From allowing yourself to be wrong.

Allowing yourself not to be good at first.

When you are young…

you don't know what you like.

A little older…

you still don't know.

Because knowing only comes from doing.

Not from thinking.

People often say…

follow your passion.

But how can you when you don't have one yet?

The answer is…

do it first.

Do whatever you are curious about.

Do whatever you find interesting.

Even just a little.

It is in the process of doing…

that you discover what you like.

Passion is like a small flame.

It needs to be nurtured.

With time.

With patience.

With small efforts every day.

No one is born with a clear passion.

Not even the most successful people.

They were once uncertain…

once lost…

once unsure of what they wanted.

What made the difference is they didn't sit still.

They took action.

They tried.

They failed.

They learned.

And from that they found their path.

If you still haven't found your passion…

don't worry.

You are not alone.

Start with a different question.

Not what do I want to do?

But what am I curious about?

Curiosity is the first step.

It leads you to experimentation.

Experimentation leads to discovery.

And discovery leads to passion.

A passion doesn't have to be grand.

It could be writing.

It could be drawing.

It could be cooking.

It could be taking care of plants.

It could be teaching.

Anything that makes you lose track of time.

Anything you would do even if you weren't paid.

That is a sign of passion.

And once you find it…

hold on tight.

Nurture it every day.

Passion will not grow if it is starved.

You can rest assured…

the journey of finding your passion…

is not a race.

It is a journey of self-discovery.

Lasting a lifetime.

And the most important thing…

is to never stop experimenting.

The world is vast.

There is so much you don't yet know.

Start today.

Start with one small thing.

And see where it leads you.
