The power of forgiveness.

There is a burden that many people carry…

that is unforgiveness.

It is heavier than any luggage.

Because it is not on your shoulders…

it is in your heart.

When someone hurts you…

you have the right to be angry.

You have the right not to forgive.

But the question is…

do you want to carry that burden forever?

Unforgiveness is like…

drinking poison…

and hoping the other person dies.

You are the one suffering.

Not them.

Every day you hold onto resentment…

you are poisoning yourself.

You are letting the past control your present.

Forgiveness is not forgetting.

It is not accepting wrongdoing.

It is not saying "it's okay"…

when things are not okay.

Forgiveness is freeing yourself.

It is saying "I choose not to let this control me anymore".

It is saying "I deserve peace".

There is a story about two prisoners…

looking through prison bars.

One saw mud.

One saw stars.

The unforgiving person…

only sees the mud of the past.

The forgiving person…

sees the sky of the future.

Forgiveness is a gift…

you give to yourself.

It does not change the past.

But it changes the future.

It opens the prison door…

and lets you walk free.

But how to forgive?

This is the hardest part.

Start by acknowledging the pain.

Do not pretend everything is fine.

Allow yourself to feel the anger.

Feel the sadness.

Cry if you need to.

Scream if you need to.

When you acknowledge the pain…

you begin to heal.

After that…

try to see from another perspective.

Not to excuse them.

But to understand.

Every person has their own pain.

The one who hurt you…

may also be hurting.

And finally…

make a decision.

Decide to forgive.

Not because they deserve it.

But because you deserve it.

You deserve to live without the burden.

You deserve peace.

Forgiveness is not an event.

It is a process.

It may take time.

You may have to forgive many times.

Every time the memory returns…

forgive again.

Gradually…

the pain will fade.

And one day…

you will wake up and feel light.

As if someone has lifted the stone off your chest.

That is the power of forgiveness.

Not to change others.

But to free yourself.

Forgive.

Not because they deserve it.

But because you deserve peace.
