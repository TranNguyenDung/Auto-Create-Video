The value of simplicity.

Modern life…
grows ever more complex.

We have more things.
Yet we are less happy.

Houses are bigger.
But affections are narrower.

Connections are more numerous.
But loneliness is greater.

We shop more.
But are less satisfied.

Why?
Because we forget the value of simplicity.

Simplicity is not poverty.
Simplicity is having enough.
Enough for your needs.
And not being bound by greed.

The ancients taught…
tri túc tiện túc.
Knowing when you have enough is enough.

That is the greatest wisdom in life.

When you know you have enough…
you no longer chase after luxuries.
No longer compare with others.
No longer feel lacking.

A simple meal with family.
An afternoon walk under the trees.
A good book with a warm cup of tea.

These things cost little.
But bring true happiness.

Zen Master Thich Nhat Hanh once said…
happiness is being present in the moment.
Nothing needs to be added.
Nothing needs to be removed.

Look at a lotus flower.
It grows from muddy water.
Yet remains pure.
Noble.
Fragrant.

It doesn't need to be a rose.
Doesn't need to be brilliantly red.
It only needs to be itself.
And that is the simplest beauty.

People are the same.
You don't need to be someone else.
You don't need to have everything.
Just live true to yourself.
Simple. Sincere. Natural.

Philosopher Henry David Thoreau…
once lived for two years in a small cabin by the woods.
He wrote…
I wanted to live deep and suck out all the marrow of life…
to put to rout all that was not life.

The result?
He found freedom.
And wrote the masterpiece Walden.

Simplicity helps us focus.
When we have fewer things…
we have more time.
More energy.
For what truly matters.

A wardrobe with fewer clothes…
helps you dress better.
A schedule with fewer tasks…
helps you live slower.
A room with fewer items…
helps your soul feel lighter.

Try decluttering.
Remove what you don't use.
Not just in your closet.
But in your mind too.

Let go of unnecessary worries.
Let go of toxic relationships.
Let go of goals that aren't yours.

Life is short.
Don't carry too much.

Walk lightly.
Live simply.
Love peacefully.

Because true happiness…
doesn't come from having more.
But from needing less.

And when you cherish the small things…
you'll find this life is already very rich.

Rich in every breath.
Rich in every glance.
Rich in a simplicity that is wonderfully profound.

That is true value.
The value of simplicity.
