How to write an impressive CV.

A thin piece of paper…

but it can change your whole life.

A CV is not just a list of experiences.

It is the story of who you are.

A story told in seconds.

Because recruiters only skim…

not read thoroughly.

So how do you make them stop?

First…

understand one thing.

Your CV is not to tell what you have done.

It is to prove…

what you can do for them.

Every line must answer one question.

That question is: why you?

Don't write generic things.

"Good communication skills"…

everyone says that.

Show them with numbers.

With specific achievements.

With the changes you have made.

One sentence with data…

is stronger than ten vague sentences.

Look through the recruiter's eyes.

What do they need?

What problem gives them a headache?

Your CV is the solution to that pain.

Don't just copy a template online.

Identical CVs…

will be rejected identically.

Personalize it.

Each company is a different story.

Tell their story right.

The first part is the most important.

Your name clear.

A brief introductory line.

Say clearly who you are…

what you do best.

And don't forget your contact information.

It sounds obvious…

but many people forget.

Organize the layout neatly.

The reader's eyes move naturally.

From left to right.

From top to bottom.

Don't make them search for information.

Put everything in its place.

Most recent experience at the top.

Highlight achievements where they are easy to see.

Important skills first.

The rest… cut it if unnecessary.

A two-page CV…

is not as good as a complete one-page CV.

Choose each word carefully.

Every word must have value.

There is no room for verbosity.

They say…

you have six seconds to make a first impression.

Six seconds…

for all your years of study and work.

So…

make those six seconds count.

An impressive CV…

is not the prettiest CV.

It is the CV that makes people want to meet you.

Write it…

and don't forget to proofread.

One small typo…

can ruin everything.

Because recruiters think…

if you are not careful with your CV…

you will be careless with their work.

So take your time.

Write it…

read it over.

Fix it…

ask someone else to read it.

And when it's complete…

send it with confidence.

Because the CV is only the first door.

But that door…

must be impressive enough for them to want to step through.

Your life is worth more than a piece of paper.

But that piece of paper…

is the ticket for you to enter the game.

Write it carefully.

Because you deserve the best opportunity.
