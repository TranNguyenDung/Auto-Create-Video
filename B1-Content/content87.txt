Lessons from the eagle.

In the world of birds…
the eagle is king.

It flies the highest.
It sees the farthest.
It lives the strongest.

And the eagle teaches us…
valuable lessons about life.

The first lesson…
the eagle does not hunt dragonflies.

The eagle can catch snakes.
Turtles.
Fish.
Even small wolves.

It doesn't waste time on small prey.
It chooses worthy targets.

You too.
Don't waste your life on trivial things.
Choose great goals.
Worthy of your potential.

The second lesson…
the eagle loves storms.

When a storm comes…
other birds find shelter.
But the eagle flies toward the storm.
It uses the strong winds…
to soar higher.

Difficulties are not to be avoided.
Difficulties are opportunities to rise.
Storms temper you.
Make you stronger.

Don't fear challenges.
Embrace them.
And use them to fly higher.

The third lesson…
the eagle has far-reaching vision.

The eagle can spot its prey…
from five kilometers away.
It sees farther than any other creature.
It focuses on its target.
Without distraction.

You also need far-reaching vision.
Don't just look at the present.
Look toward the future.
Set clear goals.
And focus on them.

The fourth lesson…
the eagle regenerates.

When it reaches forty years old…
the eagle's claws become dull.
Its wings become heavy.
Its beak becomes bent and weak.

The eagle has two choices.
Die.
Or go through a process of rebirth.

It flies up to a high mountain.
Strikes its beak against the rock until it falls off.
Waits for a new beak to grow.
Plucks out its old claws.
Waits for new claws to grow.
Plucks out each old feather.
Waits for new feathers to grow.

A painful process.
Lasting one hundred and fifty days.
But after that…
the eagle lives another thirty years.

Sometimes you need to regenerate.
Let go of old habits.
Let go of old thinking.
Even if it's painful.
To live a new life.

The fifth lesson…
the eagle teaches its young to fly.

When the eaglet is old enough…
the mother eagle breaks the nest.
She pushes the eaglet out of the nest.
So it can fly on its own.

If the eaglet is afraid…
the mother eagle flies underneath.
Catches it on her back.
And pushes it out again.

Until the eaglet can fly.

True love…
is not keeping your child in your arms.
It's teaching them to fly on their own.

You too.
Don't stay in your comfort zone.
Dare to jump out of the nest.
Your wings are strong enough.
You just haven't dared to open them.

Learn from the eagle.
Fly high.
See far.
Embrace storms.
Dare to regenerate.
And dare to be free.

Because you were born…
not to crawl on the ground.
But to soar in the sky.
