Why no one is perfect.

There is a truth that everyone knows…
but few accept.

No one in this world is perfect.

Yet we still chase perfection.
Like a mirage in the desert.
The more we chase…
the farther it gets.

Perfection is a standard that doesn't exist.
It's only a product of the imagination.

You want a perfect body?
You want a perfect job?
You want a perfect relationship?
You want a perfect life?

And you feel disappointed…
every time you realize nothing is perfect.

You look in the mirror and see flaws.
You look at your achievements and see shortcomings.
You look at yourself and see not enough.

That is the pain of perfectionism.
It doesn't make you better.
It only makes you suffer more.

But why is no one perfect?
The answer is very simple.

Because perfection is static.
But life is dynamic.

The most perfect flower…
will wither after a few days.
The most perfect work of art…
will be eroded by time.
The most perfect person…
would never need to grow again.

And that would be the saddest thing.
To never grow.
To never learn.
To never change.

Imperfection is what makes life.

Think about it…
if everything were perfect…
there would be no stories to tell.
No lessons to learn.
No scars to remember.

Old objects have stories.
Faces with wrinkles have depth.
Hearts that have been hurt know how to love.

Imperfection is a kind of beauty.
The Japanese call it wabi-sabi.
The beauty of imperfection.
The beauty of the temporary.
The beauty of incompleteness.

A cracked bowl…
mended with gold.
The crack is not to be hidden.
But to be honored.

It tells the story of the bowl's journey.
Of what it has been through.
And that is what gives it value.

People are the same.
The scars in your soul.
The failures in your past.
The painful falls.

Not to be ashamed of.
But to be proud of.
Because they prove you have lived.
You have dared to love, to try, to fail.

Stop chasing perfection.
Stop comparing yourself to others.
Stop criticizing your shortcomings.

Instead…
accept.
Accept that you are not perfect.
And that is completely okay.

You are on a journey.
Each day you can be a little better.
No need to be perfect.
Just need to progress.

Because a person who knows they are not perfect…
can humbly learn.
Can sincerely forgive.
Can love wholeheartedly.

And that is what truly matters.

So…
embrace your imperfection.
It is not a weakness.
It is part of what makes you who you are.

In this world…
no one is perfect.
There are only real people.
And real people always have cracks.

But those very cracks…
are where the light gets in.
