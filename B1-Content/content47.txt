The 80/20 principle in work.

Have you ever felt…

you work so much…

but the results are barely anything?

You spend the whole day busy…

but at the end of the day…

you ask yourself what you have actually accomplished.

There is a principle that can change that.

The 80/20 principle.

Also known as the Pareto principle.

An Italian economist…

named Vilfredo Pareto…

discovered something interesting.

80% of the land in Italy…

belonged to 20% of the population.

And this repeats in many fields.

80% of results…

come from 20% of efforts.

80% of revenue…

comes from 20% of customers.

80% of problems…

come from 20% of causes.

You see…

most results come from only a small portion of work.

So in your work…

what does that mean?

Very simple.

20% of the work you do…

creates 80% of the value.

And the remaining 80% of the work…

only creates 20% of the value.

If you know how to identify that 20%…

you can do less…

but achieve more.

It sounds counterintuitive…

but it's true.

The problem is most people…

don't know how to distinguish.

They do everything equally.

Spend the same time on every task.

They fear missing something.

They fear not doing enough.

But smart people…

don't do more.

They do better.

They know how to focus on what matters most.

So how to apply it?

First…

look back at your work.

Identify which tasks…

bring the biggest results.

It may only be a few things.

But they are the decisive ones.

Then, spend most of your time on them.

Don't spread yourself thin.

Don't do everything.

Be selective.

Learn to say no.

To unimportant tasks.

To unnecessary meetings.

To emails that can wait.

Every minute you spend on unimportant things…

is a minute you take away from important things.

Time is the only finite resource.

You cannot do everything.

But you can do the right things.

The 80/20 principle is not just a theory.

It is a way of life.

A way of thinking.

It reminds you…

that not all efforts are equal.

Some efforts bear sweet fruit.

Some efforts only create foam.

Put your energy into what deserves it.

Let go of things that only keep you busy.

And remember…

you are not paid for the hours you work.

You are paid for the value you create.

That is the 80/20 principle.

Simple but profound.

Apply it today.
