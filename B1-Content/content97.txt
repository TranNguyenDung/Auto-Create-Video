The power of commitment.

There is one thing…
that separates the successful from the unsuccessful.

Not talent.
Not luck.
Not circumstances.

It's commitment.

Commitment to your goals.
Commitment even when inspiration fades.
Commitment even when everything is against you.

Most people start with enthusiasm.
They are full of energy.
They believe this time will be different.

Then after a few weeks…
a few months…
enthusiasm fades.

They face difficulty.
They face failure.
They face boredom.

And they give up.

Not because they lack ability.
But because they lack commitment.

Commitment is not an emotion.
It doesn't come and go with your mood.

Commitment is a decision.
A decision you make…
and stand by at all costs.

When you commit…
you have no other option.

You will do it.
By any means.
No matter what happens.

Like a mountain.
It doesn't sway in the storm.
It just stands there.
And endures everything.

The power of commitment lies in persistence.
You may be slow.
But you don't stop.

One small step each day.
A little progress each day.
Until one day…
you realize you've come a very long way.

People often say…
better to do it slowly than not at all.
But I say…
better to do it slowly than to give up.

Because once you give up…
you have to start all over again.
And next time will be even harder.

But once you keep your commitment…
every day is a step forward.
Small.
Slow.
But it's still moving ahead.

Commitment is also a measure of character.
A person who doesn't keep promises to themselves…
how can they keep promises to others?

When you say I will do it…
and you do it.
You are building trust.
With others.
And with yourself.

Every time you keep a commitment…
you grow a little stronger.
Every time you break a promise to yourself…
you grow a little weaker.

Start with small commitments.
Commit to waking up early for three consecutive days.
Commit to reading for ten minutes each day.
Commit to exercising twice a week.

Don't commit to something too big at the start.
You will fail.
And failure will discourage you.

Commit to just enough.
Enough that you can keep your word.
Enough that you feel victorious.

And then…
little by little…
one small commitment at a time…
you will build tremendous strength.

The strength of reliability.
The strength of discipline.
The strength of a person who keeps their word.

Life…
is not a sprint.
It's a marathon of endurance.

The ultimate winner…
is not the fastest.
But the one who never gives up.

Commit to what you believe is right.
Commit to your dreams.
Commit to yourself.

And then…
let time prove the power of commitment.
