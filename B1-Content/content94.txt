The truth of impermanence in life.

There is a truth…
that everyone knows.
But few dare to face.

Everything changes.

Nothing lasts forever.
The flower that blooms today…
will wither tomorrow.
The person beside us today…
may leave tomorrow.
The emotion that burns today…
may cool tomorrow.

That is impermanence.

Eastern philosophy calls it the law of nature.
Everything is impermanent.
Everything is in flux.

Nothing is unchanging.

But humans always resist this.
We want to hold on.
Want everything to stay with us forever.

We cling to lovers.
Cling to youth.
Cling to fame.
Cling to possessions.

And then we suffer…
when everything slips away.

Because the root of suffering…
is attachment.

Attached to what has passed.
Attached to what doesn't belong to us.
Attached to the idea of permanence.

Look at the river.
Water flows without stopping.
Never the same even for a moment.

But the river is still beautiful.
Beautiful because of its constant movement.
Beautiful because it knows how to change.

Life is the same.
Impermanence is not a tragedy.
It's the nature of life.

Without impermanence…
the seed could not become a tree.
Without impermanence…
yesterday would never end.
And tomorrow would never come.

Impermanence is the condition that allows everything to happen.

When we understand this…
we no longer fear change.
No longer despair at loss.
No longer worry about the future.

Because we know…
everything comes and goes.
Like clouds drifting, like wind passing.
Like waves crashing then dissolving.

Learn to accept.
Like a gardener accepts the blooming and wilting of flowers.
Not too happy when flowers bloom.
Not too sad when flowers fade.

Just quietly observe.
And cherish every moment.

Loss is not an end.
Loss is part of the cycle.
The new replaces the old.
Things come and go.
Things gather and disperse.

Whoever understands impermanence…
will live more lightly.

No longer straining to hold onto what is old.
No longer tangled in regrets.
No longer suffering over things beyond reach.

Let life flow naturally.
Like a leaf on a stream.
Gently.
Serenely.
Trusting the current.

Impermanence…
is not meant to frighten us.
But to remind us to treasure every moment.

Because everything will pass.
So live fully.
Right now.
At this very moment.

No matter what tomorrow brings…
this moment is all you have.
