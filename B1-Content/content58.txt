The power of networking.

There is a famous saying…

your network is your net worth.

That means…

the people you know…

determine the opportunities you have.

Not everyone likes networking.

Many think it is fake.

Going to meet strangers and saying clichés.

But true networking…

is not like that.

True networking is building relationships.

Connecting person to person.

Giving before receiving.

Why is networking so important?

Because opportunities often come through people.

Not from advertisements.

Not from applications.

But from relationships.

A good job.

A business partner.

An investor.

A mentor.

All come through relationships.

So how do you build a network?

First…

give first.

Don't come with a mindset of what can I get.

Come with a mindset of what can I give.

Introduce someone.

Share knowledge.

Help with a problem.

When you give…

people will remember you.

And when you need…

they will be willing to help.

Second…

be genuine.

People can sense fakeness.

If you only pretend to care…

they will know.

Truly care about others.

Learn about them.

Their story.

Their passion.

Their struggles.

When you truly care…

relationships will naturally grow.

Third…

maintain connections.

Building a relationship is not a one-time thing.

It needs nurturing.

A check-in message.

A birthday wish.

A shared article.

Small but regular things.

Will make a difference.

Fourth…

expand your circle.

Don't only talk to people like you.

Meet people from different fields.

Different industries.

Different backgrounds.

Diversity brings unexpected opportunities.

New ideas.

Different perspectives.

Fifth…

be patient.

Building a network…

is like planting a garden.

It takes time.

It needs care.

Results won't come immediately.

But if you persist…

one day you will have a beautiful garden.

And remember…

the strongest network…

is not how many people you know.

But how many people truly know you.

And trust you.
