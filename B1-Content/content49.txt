How to work well in a team.

One person can go fast…

but many people can go far.

That saying is very familiar.

But few truly understand…

working in a team is not just putting people together.

Teamwork is an art.

And like any other art…

it must be learned.

It must be practiced.

In a team…

you cannot think only of yourself.

You must think of the whole team.

And so must everyone in the team.

The first thing about teamwork…

is communication.

Not everyone thinks like you.

Not everyone understands your meaning.

So speak clearly.

Don't make others guess.

One of the most common mistakes…

is thinking everyone knows what you're thinking.

They don't.

And you can't read their minds either.

So ask.

Listen.

Confirm.

Good communication is the foundation of every successful team.

The second thing…

is respecting differences.

Each person has their own strengths.

Each person has their own way of thinking.

Don't force others to be like you.

Let them be themselves.

And learn from those differences.

A strong team…

is not where everyone is the same.

It is where people complement each other.

Someone strong in ideas.

Someone strong in execution.

Someone strong in details.

All are necessary.

The third thing…

is responsibility.

When you take on a task…

complete it.

Don't let others have to remind you.

Don't let work stall because of you.

In a team…

one person's delay…

affects everyone else.

So value your commitments.

The fourth thing…

is knowing when to lead…

and when to follow.

You are not always the leader.

And you shouldn't be.

Knowing when to step back and let others shine…

is also a skill.

Knowing how to support others' decisions…

when they are in charge.

The fifth thing…

is building trust.

Trust is the glue that binds every team.

Without trust…

every process crumbles.

Be reliable.

Keep your promises.

Protect your teammates.

When there is conflict…

don't avoid it.

Face it and resolve it.

Openly but respectfully.

Conflict is not a bad thing.

It is an opportunity to understand each other better.

Only teams that are afraid to face conflict…

fall apart.

Finally, remember…

teamwork is not about the individual.

It is about us.

Not I.

But we.

When successful, share.

When failing, share the burden.

That is how a truly strong team works.
