How to build healthy habits.

Everyone wants good habits.
But few achieve them.

Not because they are weak.
But because they don't know how.

A habit is built…
not by willpower.
But by systems.

Willpower is a finite resource.
By evening, it's depleted.
But systems…
always work.

Start with something very small.
Something you are sure you can do.
So easy you cannot fail.

Not reading thirty pages a day.
But one page.
Not running five kilometers.
But stepping outside.

When the action is too small…
resistance disappears.
You don't need willpower to do something too easy.

Once you've started…
continuing becomes easier.

The two-minute rule.
Any habit…
can be reduced to two minutes.
Read for two minutes.
Meditate for two minutes.
Exercise for two minutes.

After that…
you'll naturally want to do more.

Attach the new habit to an old one.
After I drink my morning coffee…
I will read one page of a book.
After I brush my teeth at night…
I will meditate for one minute.

Tie them together.
Form a chain.
One habit triggers the next.

Arrange your environment to make it easier.
Want to exercise in the morning?
Leave your workout clothes next to the bed.
Want to eat healthy?
Put fruit on the table.
Hide junk food out of sight.

Environment matters more than willpower.
If your environment is favorable…
you'll automatically do the right thing.

Track your habits.
Mark your calendar each day you succeed.
A streak of consecutive days…
is powerful motivation.
You don't want to break the chain.

Never miss twice.
Everyone has weak days.
Missing one day is normal.
But two days…
is the start of a bad habit.

If you miss a day…
come back the very next day.
Don't blame yourself.
Don't think you've failed.
Just come back.

Persistence isn't about never failing.
It's about always returning after every failure.

Enjoy the process.
Good habits shouldn't be a prison.
They should be a joy.
If you hate running…
don't run.
Find a physical activity you love.
If a habit brings you joy…
you won't need discipline to maintain it.

Discipline is when you haven't found the joy yet.
Once you've found it…
discipline becomes love.

Build healthy habits starting today.
Just one small habit.
But do it every day.
Until it becomes instinct.

Then…
the habit is no longer a habit.
It's who you are.

And one day…
you look back.
And see you've changed.
Without even realizing.
When it all began.
