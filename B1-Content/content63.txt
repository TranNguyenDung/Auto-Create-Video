The art of public speaking.

There is a fear greater than the fear of death…

the fear of standing before a crowd.

It sounds strange.

But it's true.

When you stand on stage…

dozens, hundreds of eyes are on you.

Your body reacts.

Heart races.

Hands tremble slightly.

Voice shakes.

That is natural.

Even the greatest speakers…

have been through it.

So what is their secret?

They don't try to eliminate the fear.

They learn to live with it.

And turn it into energy.

A little nervousness…

is a catalyst for excellence.

Without it…

your speech will be flat.

So how do you give a great speech?

First…

don't think about having to speak beautifully.

Think about your message.

People don't remember how well you spoke.

They remember how they felt listening to you.

A message that touches the heart…

is worth more than a thousand flowery words.

Start strong.

A question.

A shocking statistic.

A short story.

The first three seconds…

determine whether people want to keep listening.

Structure your talk simply.

Opening – Body – Conclusion.

It sounds old.

But it works.

Say three main points.

No more.

The human brain only remembers three things.

Tell them what you're going to tell them.

Tell them.

And tell them what you've told them.

Repetition is key.

Body language matters more than words.

Don't cross your arms.

Stand straight.

Make eye contact with the audience.

Move around the stage.

Don't stand still.

Movement creates energy.

Your voice is an instrument.

Go up and down.

Emphasize.

Pause at important moments.

Silence is the most powerful weapon.

A well-timed pause…

makes everyone pay attention.

And a smile.

Don't forget to smile.

It makes you more approachable.

And it makes you less nervous.

When you make a mistake…

don't panic.

Keep going.

No one knows you forgot something…

unless you tell them.

The audience doesn't have the script.

They don't know what you planned to say.

So they also don't know you forgot.

Practice in front of a mirror.

Record your voice.

Listen back.

Fix mistakes.

Practice a hundred times…

so that on stage it flows like the first time.

But above all…

be yourself.

No one wants to hear a copy.

The audience can sense fakeness.

They want a real person…

with real emotions.

Great speakers…

are not the ones who speak the best.

But the ones who connect the deepest.

The stage is a place to give.

Don't be afraid.

Step up and speak.

Because the world needs to hear your voice.
