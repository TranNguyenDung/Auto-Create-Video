Lessons from the Vietnamese bamboo.

For generations…
bamboo has been intertwined with the Vietnamese people.

Not by chance.

Bamboo appears in every corner of life.
From the village bamboo hedge to the hammock.

From the chopstick to the flute.

But few stop…
to reflect on the lessons from bamboo.

Bamboo does not grow alone.
Bamboo grows in clusters, in thickets.

The strength of bamboo lies not in a single stalk.
But in unity.

When storms come…
individual stalks may sway.
But the whole bamboo grove still stands firm.

They lean on each other.
Support each other.
Together, they weather the storm.

The Vietnamese people are the same.
The strength of the nation…
lies not in one exceptional individual.

But in the spirit of unity.
In the sharing of many.

Bamboo has a peculiar trait.
When young…
it is supple, easy to bend.

But when old…
it becomes tough, hard to break.

Bamboo knows how to adapt.
Knows when to be soft.
Knows when to be hard.

The wise person is the same.
Not always having to be rigid.
Not always having to yield.

Know the time, know the situation.
That is wisdom.

Bamboo has a tenacious will to live.
Cut the bamboo down…
it sprouts again.
Burn the bamboo…
it still lives underground.

Waiting for the right time…
to grow green and lush again.

That is the indomitable spirit.
It can be knocked down…
but never destroyed.

The Vietnamese people carry that same spirit.
So many wars.
So many historical ups and downs.

Still standing.
Still rising.
Nothing can destroy them.

Bamboo also teaches us humility.
The bamboo stalk is round and straight…
not boastful.
Not showy.
But everyone knows its uses.

A capable person doesn't need to talk much.
Character shines on its own.

Bamboo also knows sacrifice.
Its trunk builds houses and bridges.
Its leaves feed livestock.
Its husks make handicrafts.

From beginning to end…
bamboo gives fully.

Without demanding.
Without complaining.
Quietly giving.

To live like bamboo…
is to live usefully for the world.

In raging storms…
bamboo stays green.

On barren, rocky soil…
bamboo stays green.

It doesn't choose the soil, it chooses the will to live.
It doesn't complain about circumstances, it finds a way to rise.

That is the greatest lesson from bamboo.

Amid life's turbulence…
learn from bamboo and live.

Flexible in behavior.
Firm in principles.
Persistent in adversity.
Humble in success.

And always ready to give…
no matter what position you are in.

Because the value of a person…
lies not in where they stand…
but in what they have given.
