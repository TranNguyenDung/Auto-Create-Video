Respecting differences.

The world was not made from a single color.

It is a painting of countless shades.

Each person is a unique color.

Some are bright…

some are quiet…

some are loud…

some are still.

And they are all beautiful.

Respecting differences…

is the hardest but also the most essential lesson.

Because the human instinct…

is to seek those who are like us.

To feel safe in similarity.

But growth…

comes from differences.

When you meet someone unlike you…

don't rush to judge.

Don't assume you are right and they are wrong.

Perhaps…

they see the world from a different perspective.

And that perspective…

has its own value.

Difference is not a threat.

It is an opportunity to learn.

When you open your heart to differences…

you expand your world.

A friend of a different religion…

teaches you about faith.

A friend from a different culture…

teaches you about ways of life.

A friend with different political views…

teaches you critical thinking.

Every difference is a lesson.

But respect does not mean agreeing with everything.

You can disagree.

Yet still respect their right to be different.

That is the line between tolerance and merging.

Tolerance is accepting them as they are.

Merging is losing yourself.

Be yourself…

and let others be themselves.

In a family…

parents and children can be very different.

The father likes quiet…

the child likes excitement.

Both are right.

Just different.

Respecting differences means not forcing anyone to be like you.

It means giving them space to grow in their own way.

It means loving them for who they truly are…

not for the mold you want them to fit.

In love…

difference is the spice.

If two people were exactly alike…

one of them would be unnecessary.

Difference creates complement.

You are strong in this…

I am strong in that.

Together…

we are complete.

Respecting differences is a journey.

It begins with realizing…

that you are also different.

And you also want to be respected.

Treat others…

as you wish to be treated.

The world would be so much more beautiful…

if everyone learned this lesson.

Differences do not divide us.

They make the world richer.

Cherish them.

Celebrate them.

Because a world of only one color…

is a dull world.
