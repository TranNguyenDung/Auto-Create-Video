Buddhist philosophy on happiness.

Two thousand five hundred years ago…
a man sat under a tree.
And discovered the truth about happiness.

He was the Buddha.
And his teachings…
remain as relevant today as ever.

The first thing Buddhism teaches…
happiness does not come from outside.

It is not in money.
Not in fame.
Not in status.
Not in other people.

Happiness lies within you.

You can have everything…
and still be unhappy.
You can have nothing…
and still be at peace.

The difference lies in the mind.

The second thing…
craving is the root of suffering.

When you want something…
and cannot have it…
you suffer.

When you have it…
you fear losing it…
you also suffer.

And when you lose it…
you suffer even more.

No satisfaction is permanent.
Because desires are limitless…
while the world is limited.

The solution is not to satisfy every desire.
But to master desire.

Knowing when you have enough is enough.

The third thing…
impermanence is the nature of life.

Everything changes.
Everything comes and goes.
Nothing lasts forever.

Youth passes.
Health passes.
Loved ones pass.
Even this body passes.

When you understand impermanence…
you cherish the present more.
You don't cling to anything.
You live each moment more deeply.

The fourth thing…
letting go is the path to freedom.

There are things you hold in your heart.
Resentments.
Regrets.
Hurts.

Holding onto them…
only hurts you more.

Others have moved on.
Only you remain in the past.
Letting go is not weakness.
It's freeing yourself.

The fifth thing…
compassion is true happiness.

Wishing others to be happy…
is the shortest path to your own happiness.

When you help someone…
you need nothing but the joy itself.
That joy is pure.
Selfless.
Without calculation.

That is true happiness.

The sixth thing…
meditation is the practice of happiness.

Not to achieve anything.
But to return to yourself.
Sit still.
Observe your breath.
Observe your thoughts.
Without judgment.
Without struggle.

Just awareness.

In that moment of awareness…
you find you are already complete.
Nothing more is needed.
That is happiness.

The final thing…
light your own lamp and walk.

No one can save you but yourself.
No one can make you happy but you.
The Buddha is only the guide.
The path is yours to walk.

Happiness is not a destination.
It's the way you walk the path.

Every step…
can be happiness.
Every breath…
can be peace.
Every moment…
can be enlightenment.

True happiness…
is very simple.

Just sit still.
Observe.
And realize…
you are already complete.
Right now.
Right here.
